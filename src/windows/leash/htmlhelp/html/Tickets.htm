About Tickets

The Kerberos for Windows program helps you manage your Kerberos tickets. Click the Get Ticket button and enter your principal (your Kerberos identity) and password to obtain a ticket. The ticket allows you to securely access all of the computers and services set up to authenticate you through Kerberos, until the ticket expires, without requiring you to enter your password again.
How to get tickets

If you have logged on to a Windows domain, you already have a Keberos ticket. This ticket is marked in the main window with an Import Status of "imported," "importable," or "protected."

Ticket Expiration
Your tickets are valid for a set amount of time before they expire and cannot be used again. Usually tickets are valid for around the length of a work day (e.g., 8 hours). A pop up window warns you 15, 10, and 5 minutes before your tickets expire. To add an audible alarm at the same intervals, select Expiration Alarm in the Options panel.
AdjustView
When you get your ticket, click Show Advanced Settings to view and adjust the ticket's lifetime. Note that some Kerberos configurations will not allow this.
Get Tickets help		 The main window includes a Valid Until column for each of your tickets. After the time listed in this column, the ticket will expire and you must get a new ticket to access network resources that use Kerberos.

Renewable Tickets
When you get your ticket, you have the option of flagging the ticket as renewable. Renewable tickets allow you to run batch jobs without interruption and to work through a long session without continually reentering your password.

Renewable Ticket Expiration Renewable tickets have a normal ticket lifetime, but they also have a renewable lifetime that is much longer (usually several days). Each time you renew your ticket, Kerberos resets the ticket lifetime to the length of the orginal ticket. You can renew the ticket as often as you need to (once at time or automatically) until the renewable lifetime is reached. Then you must obtain a new ticket.
Obtain Renewable Tickets When you get your ticket, click Show Advanced Settings and then select Renewable under "Flag this ticket as." Use the Renewable Until slider to adjust the tickets's renewable lifetime.
Get Tickets help
Renew Once Click the Renew button. Note that you cannot renew expired tickets even if the ticket is still within its renewable lifespan.
Renew Ticket Once help
Renew Automatically Select Automatic Ticket Renewal in the Options panel. Note that Leash must be active and running in order to renew tickets. This means that if your machine is in hibernation mode or if Leash is not running when it is time to renew your tickets, your tickets will not be renewed.
Renew Tickets Automatically help
View The View panel offers optional informational columns for your tickets. Two columns relate to renewable tickets: Renewable Until and Flags.

To see the date and time your renewable tickets expire and can no longer be renewed, click Renewable Until in the View panel. To see which of your tickets are renewable, click Flags.

Forwardable Tickets
When you get your ticket, you have the option of flagging the ticket as forwardable and proxiable. Forwardable tickets can be forwarded to the remote host when you connect via telnet, ssh, ftp, rlogin, or similar applications, so you will not need to get new tickets to use remote services.
Obtain Forwardable Tickets View
When you get your ticket, click Show Advanced Settings and then select Forwardable and Proxiable under "Flag this ticket as."
Get Ticket help		 The View panel offers optional informational columns for your tickets. Select Flags to open a column indicating which of your tickets are forwardable.

Importable (Windows domain) Tickets
Most Windows operating systems use the Kerberos protocol behind the scenes to authenticate you when you log on to a Windows domain with a Windows Logon session. This means that if you have logged on to a Windows domain (not just to your computer), Windows probably issued you a Kerberos ticket.

Some applications that use Kerberos accept tickets issued through a Windows Logon session. Others need to use the Kerberos for Windows interface. In most installations, Kerberos for Windows will attempt to automatically import any tickets already issued to you. Imported tickets can take full advantage of the Kerberos for Windows interface.

Windows domain tickets obtained through your Windows Logon session are automatically renewed until you exit your Windows Logon session.
Import Status If you have tickets obtained through a Windows Logon session, the main window of Kerberos for Windows shows the Import Status for all of your tickets. You can hide or show this column with the View panel checkboxes. Tickets originally obtained through a Windows Logon session are imported or importable to Kerberos for Windows, or they are protected from being imported.
Import Status meanings
Import/Don't Import Tickets You can turn the Automatic Import option on or off by clicking the Automatic Import checkbox in the Option panel. You can also import tickets directly by selecting Import Tickets in the More panel.
Import Tickets help
Renew Imported Tickets If the Automatic Import option is selected, you do not need to renew your imported tickets as they will automatically be renewed through your Windows Logon session and re-imported into Kerberos for Windows.
Destroy Imported Tickets If the Automatic Import option is selected, you cannot directly destroy imported tickets. They are destroyed when you exit your Windows Logon session.
