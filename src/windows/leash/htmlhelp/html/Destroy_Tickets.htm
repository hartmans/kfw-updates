Destroy Tickets

Destroy your tickets when you no longer need them to increase security. Once the tickets are destroyed they can no longer be used by anyone.

How to...
  • Destroy tickets obtained with the Get Ticket window
  • Destroy tickets obtained with a Windows Logon session
  • Destroy tickets automatically

Destroy obtained with the Get Ticket window

To immediately destroy all of the tickets obtained with the Get Ticket window:

  1. Click the Destroy Ticket button in the Home tab or use the keyboard shortcut [Ctrl + d].
  2. Click Okay to confirm that you want to destroy the tickets.

Note: If you have tickets with an Import Status of imported, importable, or protected, see the next help section: Destroy tickets obtained with a Windows Logon session

Destroy tickets obtained with a Windows Logon session

MIT Kerberos displays all of your tickets, including those obtained when you logged on to a Windows domain with a Windows Logon session. Tickets obtained with a Windows Logon session show an Import Status of imported, importable, or protected.
About importable (Windows domain) tickets

Destroy imported tickets

Imported tickets cannot be destroyed in MIT Kerberos if the Automatic Import option is selected, because Kerberos would immediately re-import the destroyed ticket. However, they are automatically destroyed when you exit your Windows Logon session.

If you do want to immediately destroy only the imported copy:

  1. Open the Options tab.
  2. Deselect the Automatic Import Tickets checkbox in the Ticket Options panel. Using the Option Panel
  3. Click the Destroy Ticket button.

Destroy importable or protected tickets

Importable and protected tickets are not controlled by MIT Kerberos so you cannot use MIT Kerberos to destroy them. They are destroyed when you log out of your Windows session.

Destroy tickets automatically

To automatically destroy your tickets when you exit MIT Kerberos:

  1. Open the Options tab.
  2. Find the Destroy Tickets on Exit checkbox in the Ticket Options panel. If it is not already selected, click it to turn the option on.

Your tickets are not affected immediately, but they will be automatically destroyed as soon as you exit MIT Kerberos.

Note: Tickets that were obtained when you logged on to a Windows domain though a Windows Logon session are not affected by the Destroy Tickets on Exit option. They are destroyed when you exit your Windows Logon session.
